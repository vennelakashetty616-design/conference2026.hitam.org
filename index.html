About the Conference

The National Conference on Emerging and Sustainable Technologies aims to bring together students, researchers, academicians, and industry professionals to discuss innovations that drive sustainability in mechanical and allied engineering fields.

Purpose

Provide a platform for sharing research, best practices, and technological advances that contribute to sustainable development.

Importance

Emerging technologies are reshaping manufacturing, energy, materials, and automation‚Äîensuring efficiency, resilience, and environmental responsibility.

Benefits

  • Innovation & sustainability
  • Research dissemination
  • Industry‚Äìacademia interaction
  • Knowledge sharing

Who Should Attend

UG/PG students, research scholars, faculty, and industry professionals interested in sustainable technologies and mechanical engineering innovations.

About HITAM

Hyderabad Institute of Technology and Management (HITAM)

HITAM is a forward-looking institution committed to outcome-based education, hands-on learning, and strong industry engagement. The campus offers contemporary infrastructure, well-equipped laboratories, and a supportive learning environment that fosters innovation and entrepreneurship.

  • Academic Excellence: Outcome-driven teaching and continuous improvement
  • Infrastructure: Modern labs, centers of excellence, collaborative spaces
  • Industry Connect: Projects, internships, and mentorship programs
  • Holistic Development: Clubs, chapters, and community initiatives

Mechanical Engineering

Mechanical Engineering Department

The Department of Mechanical Engineering nurtures engineers with strong fundamentals and contemporary skills in design, manufacturing, materials, thermal systems, and automation. The department emphasizes sustainability, research, and industry-readiness.

  • Vision: Develop competent and ethical mechanical engineers for a sustainable future
  • Mission: Impart foundational knowledge, practical skills, and research mindset
  • Expertise: Manufacturing, materials & composites, thermal, CAD/CAM, robotics
  • Research Focus: Green technologies, smart manufacturing, advanced materials
  • Student Achievements: Projects, publications, hackathons, design competitions

Conference Tracks

Track 1: Sustainable Energy Solutions & Clean Power Technologies (MECH + EEE)

Focus: Practical energy solutions for a low-carbon society.

  • Clean and renewable energy; efficient thermal systems and heat recovery
  • Electric and hybrid mobility; storage for grid and off-grid
  • Smart, resilient power systems and waste-to-energy options
  • Energy audits and optimization techniques

Track chairs: Dr. O. P. Suresh; Mr. K. Suresh; Dr. Ruchir Shrivastava

Track 2: Smart Electronics & Sensor-Based Solutions for Society (ECE + IoT)

Focus: Electronics that directly improve quality of life.

  • Sensor-based monitoring for health, agriculture, and environment
  • Embedded systems for energy and water management
  • IoT for smart villages and smart cities; assistive and wearable tech
  • Low-power electronics and sensing for disaster management

Track chairs: Dr. J. Rajeshwar Goud; Dr. S. V. Devika; Dr. Rohit

Track 3: Software Systems & Cyber Solutions for Sustainable Development (CSE + CS)

Focus: Software as an enabler of societal transformation.

  • Software for sustainable infrastructure and smart governance
  • Cyber security for critical societal systems
  • Cloud and edge solutions; secure digital public platforms
  • Green computing and energy-aware software

Track chairs: Dr. S. V. Hemanth; Dr. K. David Raju

Track 4: AI & Data-Driven Solutions for Societal Challenges (AI-ML + DS)

Focus: Intelligence for real-world impact.

  • AI-based decision support for sustainability
  • Data-driven solutions for energy, health, and mobility
  • Predictive analytics for climate and resource management
  • Ethical and responsible AI for smart cities and social development

Track chairs: Dr. Rohit; Dr. Aparna

Track 5: Cyber-Physical Systems & Intelligent Automation for Smart Society (IoT + ECE + CSE)

Focus: Integrating physical systems with intelligence.

  • Cyber-physical systems for smart infrastructure and utilities
  • Intelligent automation and robotics for healthcare, agriculture, logistics
  • Digital twins for sustainable system design; smart transportation
  • Industry 4.0 with a sustainability focus

Track chairs: Dr. P. Padmaja; Mr. Bhaskar Das; Mr. Satyanarayana

Track 6: Sustainable Materials, Applied Physics & Engineering Innovations (Physics + MECH)

Focus: Materials as enablers of sustainable engineering.

  • Sustainable and eco-friendly materials; resource-efficient manufacturing
  • Advanced materials for energy and mobility; applied physics solutions
  • Composite and functional materials for sustainability
  • Material performance, durability, and lifecycle analysis

Track chairs: Dr. Motilal Lakavat; Dr. Jagga Rao; Dr. U. M. Singh

Track 7: Integrated Engineering Solutions for Sustainable Society (Interdisciplinary)

Focus: Holistic, cross-domain solutions.

  • Interdisciplinary engineering solutions for societal problems
  • Smart city and rural development; climate resilience
  • Real-world case studies and industry-academia collaborations
  • Policy, ethics, and sustainability in engineering education

Track chairs: Dr. T. Rambabu; Dr. B. V. R. S. Subrahmanyam; Dr. Shraddha

Submission Guidelines

Abstract submission is MANDATORY for both paper and poster presentations.

  • Word Limit: 250-300 words
  • Format: Should include Title, Authors, Keywords (3-5), and Brief Content
  • Language: English only
  • Content: Should clearly state problem statement, methodology, and expected outcomes
  • Deadline: Check Important Dates section

Important: Abstract submission is required before registration.
